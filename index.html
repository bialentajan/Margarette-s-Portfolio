RESEARCH

TITLE: Unmasking the Myth: Why Smoking Fails as a Long-Term Stress Reliever

RESEARCH PAPER LINK

REFLECTION

    Our research explored whether smoking truly serves as a long-term stress reliever. We used theories from James J. Gross and Leon Festinger to better understand why people continue smoking despite knowing the risks. We discovered that smoking offers only short-term relief. In fact, it creates more stress and dependency over time, so it’s not the healthy coping strategy many believe it to be. Through interviews and analysis, we noticed that young adults still smoke despite knowing the risks. It was revealing to see how something that seems like a quick fix can make life more difficult in the long run. This study made me realize that we really need better and healthier ways to handle stress. One of the biggest challenges I faced in this subject was working with teammates who weren’t always cooperative and dealing with the small mistakes that came up during activities. I managed to overcome these by becoming more organized and prepared, which helped group work run more smoothly and effectively. Throughout this process, I gained many valuable skills in research, leadership, and communication. I also expanded my vocabulary and understanding of different concepts. I know these skills will help me as I pursue my dream of becoming a doctor. At the same time, I realize I still need to improve in areas like procrastination, leadership, and organization. I plan to work on better time management, setting priorities, and actively participating in group activities to build my discipline and responsibility.
